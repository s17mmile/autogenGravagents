Combined summary of comment trends:
Across all summaries, the same broad picture emerges:

- **5n is the strongest family overall**, with **5n-F and 5n-B** consistently receiving the best feedback.
- **41m is the middle tier**: often substantively decent, but disproportionately affected by strict or inconsistent grading on rounding, units, notation, and formatting.

### Overall pair-wise pattern
- **5n-F**: usually the best on deep reasoning, caveats, ambiguity handling, cross-checks, and scientific robustness. Its main downside is occasional over-elaboration or failure to commit cleanly to the exact benchmark-style final answer.
- **5n-B**: usually the best \u201cclean benchmark solver\u201d \u2014 concise, accurate, and highly reliable. Slightly less rich than 5n-F, but often better aligned to the expected final answer format.
- **41m-F / 41m-B**: generally competent and often close to correct, but repeatedly judged harshly for tiny numerical differences, equivalent unit choices, or alternative formatting. 41m-F is often somewhat more complete than 41m-B, but not consistently better in judged outcomes.
- **4om-B / 4om-F**: often identify the right general method and produce polished-looking reasoning, but repeatedly fail in execution \u2014 arithmetic slips, sign mistakes, unit conversion errors, wrong assumptions, incomplete endings, or wrong final numbers. Flexible mode improves exposition more than accuracy.

### Common praise themes
Across the stronger outputs, evaluators repeatedly praised:
- clear step-by-step derivations
- correct formula selection
- unit discipline
- sign-convention awareness
- cross-checks / substitutions / validation
- physical interpretation and explicit assumptions

These strengths were concentrated most strongly in **5n**, then to a lesser extent in **41m**.

### Common criticism themes
Across weaker or lower-rated outputs, the same issues recur:
- correct method but wrong final arithmetic
- sign errors
- unit conversion mistakes
- rounding / formatting mismatches treated as wrong
- incomplete final answer despite good setup
- overcomplication that drifts from the requested quantity
- wrong physical/modeling assumptions on subtle problems

These were most common for **4om**, while **41m** more often suffered from \u201calmost right but marked wrong\u201d issues.

### Basic vs Flexible trend
A strong consensus appears here too:
- For **5n**, both modes are strong; **B** is cleaner and more benchmark-aligned, while **F** adds useful depth, caveats, and ambiguity handling.
- For **41m**, flexible mode gives mixed benefits: sometimes better structure, but also more opportunities for mismatch or stricter grading.
- For **4om**, flexible mode usually **does not fix core numerical weaknesses**; it often adds verbosity without improving correctness.

### Evaluator/reference consistency is a major meta-theme
One of the strongest cross-summary conclusions is that the **evaluation process itself was not fully consistent**.

Recurring issues include:
- tiny rounding differences sometimes accepted, sometimes rejected
- equivalent units or notation inconsistently treated
- some comments appearing contradictory or overly literal
- several reference answers seeming ambiguous, flawed, or physically questionable

A particularly important pattern is that **5n was more often credited for substantive correctness**, including when it challenged flawed references, whereas **41m and 4om were more often judged by exact reference matching**, even when the reference itself may have been dubious.

### Best overall synthesis
- **Most reliable overall:** the **5n family**
- **Best deep analytical performer:** **5n-F**
- **Best concise answer-key-aligned performer:** **5n-B**
- **Middle but evaluation-noisy:** **41m-F / 41m-B**
- **Weakest due to execution fragility:** **4om-F / 4om-B**

### Final conclusion
Taken together, the summaries show that performance differences are driven by two things:
1. **true model reliability**, where 5n > 41m > 4om
2. **evaluation noise/strictness**, which especially distorted the apparent performance of **41m** and sometimes penalized scientifically valid non-reference-aligned answers
