Problem Description:
Benzoic acid, $1.35 \mathrm{~g}$, is reacted with oxygen in a constant volume calorimeter to form $\mathrm{H}_2 \mathrm{O}(l)$ and $\mathrm{CO}_2(g)$ at $298 \mathrm{~K}$. The mass of the water in the inner bath is $1.55 \times$ $10^3 \mathrm{~g}$. The temperature of the calorimeter and its contents rises $2.76 \mathrm{~K}$ as a result of this reaction. Calculate the calorimeter constant.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Reference Explanation:


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Final Answer:
6.64  $10^3 \mathrm{~J}^{\circ} \mathrm{C}^{-1}$
