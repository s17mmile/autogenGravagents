			Problem Description: Find the area of the region enclosed by the parabolas y=x^2 and y=2 x-x^2
			Reference Explanation: 
We first find the points of intersection of the parabolas by solving their equations simultaneously. This gives $x^2=2 x-x^2$, or $2 x^2-2 x=0$. Thus $2 x(x-1)=0$, so $x=0$ or 1 . The points of intersection are $(0,0)$ and $(1,1)$. By analyzing these two equations, we can see that the top and bottom boundaries are
$$
y_T=2 x-x^2 \quad \text { and } \quad y_B=x^2
$$
The area of a typical rectangle is
$$
\left(y_T-y_B\right) \Delta x=\left(2 x-x^2-x^2\right) \Delta x
$$
and the region lies between $x=0$ and $x=1$. So the total area is
$$
\begin{aligned}
A & =\int_0^1\left(2 x-2 x^2\right) d x=2 \int_0^1\left(x-x^2\right) d x \\
& =2\left[\frac{x^2}{2}-\frac{x^3}{3}\right]_0^1=2\left(\frac{1}{2}-\frac{1}{3}\right)=\frac{1}{3}
\end{aligned}
$$
