			Reference Explanation: 
The mass loss $\Delta m$ in an interval $\Delta t$ is related to the collision flux by
$$
\Delta m=Z_{\mathrm{W}} A_0 m \Delta t
$$
where $A_0$ is the area of the hole and $m$ is the mass of one atom. It follows that
$$
Z_{\mathrm{W}}=\frac{\Delta m}{A_0 m \Delta t}
$$
Because $Z_{\mathrm{W}}$ is related to the pressure by eqn $$
Z_{\mathrm{W}}=\frac{p}{(2 \pi m k T)^{1 / 2}}
$$, we can write
$$
p=\left(\frac{2 \pi R T}{M}\right)^{1 / 2} \frac{\Delta m}{A_0 \Delta t}
$$
