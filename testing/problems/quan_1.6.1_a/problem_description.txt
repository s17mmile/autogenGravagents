Problem Description:
A one-particle, one-dimensional system has $\Psi=a^{-1 / 2} e^{-|x| / a}$ at $t=0$, where $a=1.0000 \mathrm{~nm}$. At $t=0$, the particle's position is measured. Find the probability that the measured value lies between $x=1.5000 \mathrm{~nm}$ and $x=1.5001 \mathrm{~nm}$.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Reference Explanation:

In this tiny interval, $x$ changes by only $0.0001 \mathrm{~nm}$, and $\Psi$ goes from $e^{-1.5000} \mathrm{~nm}^{-1 / 2}=0.22313 \mathrm{~nm}^{-1 / 2}$ to $e^{-1.5001} \mathrm{~nm}^{-1 / 2}=0.22311 \mathrm{~nm}^{-1 / 2}$, so $\Psi$ is nearly constant in this interval, and it is a very good approximation to consider this interval as infinitesimal. The desired probability is given as
$$
\begin{aligned}
|\Psi|^2 d x=a^{-1} e^{-2|x| / a} d x & =(1 \mathrm{~nm})^{-1} e^{-2(1.5 \mathrm{~nm}) /(1 \mathrm{~nm})}(0.0001 \mathrm{~nm}) \\
& =4.979 \times 10^{-6}
\end{aligned}
$$



--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Correct Final Answer:
4.979  $10^{-6}$
